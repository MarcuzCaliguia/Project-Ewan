WHAT EXACTLY IS COMPUTER SCIENCE

The Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS) is a four-year degree course that encompasses computing theories, ideas, and foundational algorithms. The curriculum equips students with the skills required to design and build algorithmically omplicated software as well as to bring new, efficient solutions to computational issues. Although knowing how to program is essential to the study of computer science, it is only one element of the field. Computer scientists design and analyze algorithms to solve programs and study the performance of computer hardware and software. The problems that computer scientists encounter range from the abstract-- determining what problems can be solved with computers and the complexity of the algorithms that solve them – to the tangible – designing applications that perform well on handheld devices, that are easy to use, and that uphold security measures.

WHAT CAN YOU EXPECT TO LEARN IN COMPUTER SCIENCE COURSE?

While programming is a prerequisite for pursuing computer science, it is simply one aspect of the field. When attending university, students can also acquire up and grasp the following expertise:
1. Understanding of a range of programming languages
2. Understanding of computing architecture, construction, engineering and design
3. Analytical and critical thinking skills
4. Awareness of and ability to cope with and adapt to rapid technological changes
5. Excellent mathematics/numeracy skills
6. Ability to understand complex numerical data
7. Creative thinking skills
